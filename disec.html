UNGA: DISEC

The prevention of the militarization and weaponisation of outer space with special emphasis on nuclear weapons


Director: Prakhar Agarwal

Vice Directors: Mohammad Darvish and Aaryan Singla


Since the start of the Cold War, the two superpowers, i.e. United States and USSR, competed with one another to have the ‘upper hand’ and assert their ideological differences and martial dominance over both, the world, and one another. This approach escalated to a higher level, and into the world of technology and astronomy, thus leading to the Space Race. Whilst this power-struggle has led to immense technological advancements, it has also made space vulnerable to the spread of debris and playing the role of an ideal arsenal for nations to arm themselves. The armament of space imposes a looming threat to the entire world. Both, the weaponization and the militarization of space make the satellites and spacecrafts in the LEO and GEO zones susceptible to the spread of debris and the likelihood of Kessler Syndrome bounding the mankind from ever progressing themselves into the cosmos. Thus, DISEC calls for nations to cooperate, amalgamate ideas, and exercise strong progressive altruistic actions to encourage mass disarmament of outer space to avoid a much-likely extra-terrestrial war in the near future.
