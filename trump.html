White House Situation Room

Kuwait Crisis


Director: Mohit Charan

Vice Directors: Rehan Kapur


The Invasion of Kuwait on 2 August 1990 was a 2-day operation conducted by Iraq against the neighbouring state of Kuwait, which resulted in the seven-month-long Iraqi occupation of the country. This invasion and Iraq's subsequent refusal to withdraw from Kuwait by a deadline mandated by the United Nations led to military intervention by a United Nations-authorized coalition of forces led by the United States. These events came to be known as the first Gulf War and resulted in the expulsion of Iraqi forces from Kuwait and the Iraqis setting 600 Kuwaiti oil wells on fire during their retreat. The Kuwait crisis is one of the most debated topics in the world but when you see through the eyes of the United States, it’s a whole new spectrum.

This committee would be focusing on problem solving and would require delegates to be on their feet throughout as it would be a continuous crisis. With war threats, to peace treaties, to press releases; the White House Situation room will have it all.
